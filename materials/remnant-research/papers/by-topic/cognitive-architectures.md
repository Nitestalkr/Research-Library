## Foundational Theory

### [Global Workspace Theory (GNWT)](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1364661300015394)

- **Authors:** Dehaene, Naccache
- **Published:** 2001
- **Relevance:** GNW (foundational)
- **Framework Score:** 10/10

The original broadcast model that GNW's drive-competition mechanism is built on. Drive scores compete for "broadcast" via the priority matrix — directly analogous to GNWT's workspace access model where specialized processors compete for global broadcast access.

---

### [IIT — Integrated Information Theory](https://bmcneurosciences.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1186/1471-2202-5-42)

- **Authors:** Tononi
- **Published:** 2008
- **Relevance:** GNW (integration constraints)
- **Framework Score:** 8/10

Informs integration and coherence requirements in the cognitive cycle. The stability constraints (memory integrity, conflict convergence) draw on IIT's emphasis on integrated, non-decomposable processing. A GNW drive coalition must be informationally integrated before broadcast.

---

### [Attention Schema Theory](https://academic.oup.com/book/25561)

- **Authors:** Graziano
- **Published:** 2013
- **Relevance:** GNW (self-modeling)
- **Framework Score:** 7/10

Informs the self-awareness dimension of the boredom formula and the drive system's capacity to model its own state. The agent's schema of its own attention is what enables drive-priority self-monitoring.

---

### [Predictive Processing](https://www.fil.ion.ucl.ac.uk/~karl/The%20free-energy%20principle%20-%20a%20unified%20brain%20theory.pdf)

- **Authors:** Friston
- **Published:** 2010
- **Relevance:** GNW + GRAO
- **Framework Score:** 9/10

Free-energy principle underlies both GNW's adaptive drive state estimation and GRAO's gradient-driven update logic. Prediction-error signals map directly to GRAO directional gradients.
